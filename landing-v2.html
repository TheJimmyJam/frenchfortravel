THE PROBLEM WITH LANGUAGE LEARNING

Most apps assume English is the center of the language-learning world. You end up translating everything twice. That's not how fluency works.

01 Duolingo and competitors are English-first — even when you're a Spanish speaker learning French, you're routed through English definitions.
02 Vocabulary without context doesn't stick — isolated word drills miss the grammatical bridges that actually build fluency.
03 Conversations are what matter — but most apps skip real-world scenarios like cafés, airports, and workplaces until you're "advanced enough."
04 There's no path — random lessons without a 30-day structure leave most learners stuck at "bonjour" for six months.

Without native context, you're always translating.
