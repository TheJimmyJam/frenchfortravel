# The Cunning Linguist — 30-Day Plan

A structured curriculum for total beginners aiming for café-confidence in Paris. 20–30 minutes per day. Pair this with the interactive web app (`index.html`) and the vocab tracker (`Vocab-Tracker.xlsx`).

---

## How to Use This Plan

1. **Open `index.html`** each morning. It shows today's lesson with vocab, phrases, grammar, and practice.
2. **Read the lesson** and say each word/phrase out loud (5–10 minutes).
3. **Drill flashcards** in the app (5–10 minutes). Mark "Got it" or "Need work".
4. **Quiz yourself** at the end of each session (5 minutes).
5. **Review a conversation scenario** — read both parts aloud (5 minutes).
6. **Mark the day complete** in the app to advance.

Skip a day? No big deal. The streak resets but the learned vocab stays. Pick up where you left off.

## After Day 30

You're ready for Paris. Some next steps:

- **Keep the app open** — flashcard mode against all 30 days, weekly.
- **Watch French content** — short YouTube clips with subtitles. Search "French slow news" or "Easy French".
- **Listen on the way to work** — Coffee Break French (podcast) is excellent.
- **Start a conversation** — italki or Preply for cheap 1:1 video lessons. Even one a week makes a difference.
- **Talk to yourself** — narrate your day in French. Sounds silly. Works.

## Realistic Expectations

After 30 days at 20–30 min/day, you will:

- ✅ Greet people and start conversations politely
- ✅ Order food and drinks at any café or restaurant
- ✅ Check into a hotel and handle minor issues
- ✅ Ask for and follow directions
- ✅ Buy things, ask prices, decline politely
- ✅ Get help in an emergency
- ✅ Make small talk with patient locals

You will NOT:

- ❌ Be fluent
- ❌ Understand fast native speech
- ❌ Watch French TV without subtitles
- ❌ Read French novels easily

That's fine. The goal here is **competent traveler**, not fluent speaker. The single most important phrase you'll learn is `Bonjour, parlez-vous anglais ?` — and the willingness to try.

---

## The Bonjour Rule

If you remember nothing else from this plan, remember this: **always say "Bonjour" first.** Walking into a shop in Paris and immediately asking a question in English (or even in French!) without saying Bonjour is considered rude. It's the single biggest difference between travelers who get warm service and travelers who feel "Parisians are rude."

Just say `Bonjour` first. Wait for the `Bonjour` back. Then proceed.
